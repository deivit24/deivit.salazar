Bio

A Little more about me

I attended Lipscomb University in Nashville on a full-tennis scholarship and graduated with 150hrs in Accounting. I was ready to sit for my CPA exam. However, I wanted to follow my passion, tennis. I was fortunate enough to become an assistant Men’s Tennis Coach at the United States Military Academy at West Point. After my short tenure there, I was offered a scholarship to attend Durham University in the United Kingdom to play tennis and pursue a Master’s Degree.

After receiving my Master’s in Finance Management, I moved to St. Louis, MO to work at one of the biggest financial service firms in the country, Edward Jones. I was relocated to Austin, TX and worked as a Financial Advisor. Although I found success fast, the industry I was working in was very cut-throat and I found many conflicts of interests between the firms and advisors. This lead to quit my job and start my own Registered Investment Advisor Firm, Armis Financial LLC. This way, I continue helping out my clients without pressure from HQ and without tarnishing my values and moral compass.

I always spent to much time behind the computer since Windows 95 came out. I was intrigued by them. So much so that I almost considered switching Majors my senior year in college. It wasn’t till I started dabbling with the idea about having my own business that I really started to code. I knew I needed a unique and responsive website and I did not want to pay someone to do it or have an unoriginal template. So, I started learning HTML and CSS. I had an epiphany. I started learning new technologies and programing language day in and day out. I even considered applying for a coding boot camp but after my due diligence, I started learning on my own. I read books, spent hours on YouTube and Udemy and going to meet ups. I am now comfortable with my own skills to say that I am Front-End Web Developer. I strive to learn something new everyday and aspire to be a Full-Stack Web-Developer someday as well.
